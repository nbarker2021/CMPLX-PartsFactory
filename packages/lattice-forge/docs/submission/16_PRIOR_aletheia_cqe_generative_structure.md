## WHY 6-10, NOT EXACTLY 10

Observability Depends on Dimension

In 1D (strings), you only see the triad and its mirror: 6 elements. The bonds (7-8) collapse because there’s only one axis. The invariant (9) is implicit (360° closure). The gate (10) is whether the string closes.

In 2D, you see 8: the triad, the mirror, and both bonds become visible as orthogonal directions.

In 3D (the classical Oloid), you see the bonds clearly as the two circles. The invariant is the surface-area-equals-sphere constraint.

In 8D (E8), all 10 are distinguishable. The full structure is visible.

The 6-10 range: you always have at least 6 (triad + mirror). You have at most 10 (triad + mirror + bonds + invariant + gate). How many are OBSERVABLY DISTINCT depends on the dimensionality of your context. Low dimensions collapse some into each other. High dimensions separate all of them.

The 360° Closure in Strings (1D Case)

In 1D, a string that closes on itself must complete 360°. The closure condition IS the gate (element 10). The string’s tension IS the invariant (element 9). The two endpoints being the same point makes bonds A and B identical (elements 7 and 8 collapse to one). The string’s direction and reverse are the mirror pair (elements 1-3 and 4-6 are each visible but the distinction between entity/void/relation is compressed).

Derivation: From 360° closure alone, you can derive: 360 = 6 × 60 (6 equilateral triangles tile the plane). Each triangle is a triad. Closing 6 triads gives 360°. This is the hexagonal tiling — the 2D analog of E8’s sphere packing. The 6 minimum generating elements correspond to the 6 triangles needed for 360° closure.

The Doubling Chain Produces the Range

Your observation: 1 → 2 → 4 → 8 → ∞. This doubling is present in E8’s Dynkin diagram arm lengths: 1, 2, 4 (= 2⁰, 2¹, 2²). The doubling STOPS at 4 because E8 is rank 8 = 2³, and 2⁰ + 2¹ + 2² + 1(branch) = 8.

But the doubling in the generative count goes: 1 → 3 (not 2!) → 6 (= 3×2) → 9 (= 3×3) → 10 (= 3×3 + 1). The factor of 3 (triad) replaces the factor of 2 (duality) as the fundamental generator. This is the key refinement: the Oloid’s duality (2 circles) is SECONDARY to the triad’s ternary structure (entity/void/relation).
